LLT provides control over how and how much your battery charges, allowing you to optimize for long-term battery health, everyday use, or fast top-ups. You can also view detailed battery statistics and configure overnight charging behavior.

## Charging Modes

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Conservation" icon="leaf">
    Charges only to a firmware-set threshold (~60% for MY2021 and earlier, ~80% for MY2022 and later). Best for when the laptop stays plugged in most of the time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Normal" icon="battery-half">
    Standard charging behavior. Charges the battery to 100% at a regular rate. Suitable for daily use when you need a full charge.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Rapid Charge" icon="battery-full">
    Charges the battery faster than Normal mode. Useful when you need a quick top-up before heading out.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Conservation Mode Threshold

<Warning>
  The Conservation mode charge threshold is set in firmware and **cannot be changed** through LLT or any other software. The ceiling is approximately **60%** on MY2021 laptops and earlier, and approximately **80%** on MY2022 laptops and later.
</Warning>

This threshold is enforced by the embedded controller (EC) and is not configurable. If you require a different charge ceiling, Conservation mode may not suit your needs.

## Night Charge

Night Charge (`BatteryNightChargeState`) lets you schedule the laptop to charge during off-peak hours — for example, completing a full charge only in the early morning rather than keeping the battery at 100% all night.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Night Charge: On">
    The system delays reaching a full charge until closer to when you typically wake or start using the device. This reduces the time the battery spends sitting at 100%, which can degrade cells over time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Night Charge: Off">
    Standard charging resumes. The battery charges to its ceiling (100% in Normal/Rapid, or the firmware threshold in Conservation) as soon as possible when plugged in.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  For the best long-term battery health, consider combining **Conservation mode** during the day with **Night Charge** enabled overnight. This keeps the battery from sitting at 100% for extended periods while still ensuring a usable charge is ready in the morning.
</Tip>

## Battery Statistics

LLT can display battery statistics sourced from the Windows battery reporting API. The statistics view shows data about the current battery condition, giving you insight into degradation over time.

Typical statistics visible include:

- **Design capacity** — the capacity the battery was rated for when new
- **Full charge capacity** — the maximum charge the battery can currently hold
- **Battery wear** — the difference between design and full charge capacity, expressed as a percentage

<Note>
  Battery statistics are read-only and reflect data reported by the Windows battery subsystem. Accuracy depends on the firmware and driver stack, and values can vary slightly over a charge cycle.
</Note>

## Command Line

Battery charging mode can be read and set via the LLT CLI:

```sh
# List available charging modes
llt feature set Battery --list

# Get the current charging mode
llt feature get Battery

# Switch to Conservation mode
llt feature set Battery Conservation

# Switch to Rapid Charge
llt feature set Battery RapidCharge
```

CLI requires LLT to be running in the background and the CLI option to be enabled in LLT settings.
