Lenovo Legion Toolkit (LLT) gives you full control over your Lenovo Legion laptop without the bloat of Lenovo Vantage or Legion Zone. Change power modes, manage battery health, configure RGB lighting, set up automated actions, and control your GPU — all from a single lightweight app.

LLT runs no background services, uses virtually no CPU, and contains no telemetry.

## What LLT does

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Power Modes" icon="gauge-high">
    Switch between Quiet, Balance, Performance, and Custom (God Mode) with full fan curve control.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Battery Management" icon="battery-three-quarters">
    Set Conservation, Normal, or Rapid Charge modes to protect long-term battery health.
  </Card>
  <Card title="GPU Control" icon="microchip">
    Manage Hybrid Mode, deactivate idle dGPU, and overclock your NVIDIA GPU.
  </Card>
  <Card title="RGB Lighting" icon="lightbulb">
    Configure Spectrum per-key RGB and 4-zone RGB backlight with hardware-native effects.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Automation" icon="robot">
    Create action pipelines that respond to AC power, games launching, lid events, and more.
  </Card>

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download LLT">
    Grab the latest installer from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/BartoszCichecki/LenovoLegionToolkit/releases/latest) or install via `winget install BartoszCichecki.LenovoLegionToolkit`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check compatibility">
    LLT supports Legion Gen 6–9 (MY2021–2024) laptops. See the [compatibility guide](/compatibility) for details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure autorun">
    Go to **Settings** and enable **Autorun** and **Minimize on close** so LLT always runs in the background.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disable Vantage">
    In **Settings**, disable Lenovo Vantage, Legion Zone, and Hotkeys to avoid conflicts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
LLT must be running in the background for some features to work — including power mode sync with Windows, Macros, and Actions. Enable **Autorun** in Settings to ensure this.
</Note>
